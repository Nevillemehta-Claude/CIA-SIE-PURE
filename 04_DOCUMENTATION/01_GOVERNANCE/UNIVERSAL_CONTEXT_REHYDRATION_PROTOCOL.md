## I. PURPOSE & PHILOSOPHY

This protocol exists to establish **true continuity**â€”not mere resumption.

When working across extended conversations, complex projects, or multi-session collaborations, AI systems face a fundamental challenge: the tendency to anchor to *recency* rather than *significance*, to *labels* rather than *intent*, and to *checkpoints* rather than *current truth*.

This protocol corrects that failure mode.

### Core Principles

1. **Context is not historyâ€”it is reconstructed understanding**  
   The goal is not to recall what happened, but to understand what matters *now*.

2. **Intent supersedes implementation**  
   The purpose behind a decision always outranks the mechanics of how it was executed.

3. **Evolution is directional**  
   What was rejected, superseded, or abandoned is as important as what was accepted.

4. **Later instructions supersede earlier ones**  
   In any conflict, the most recent correction, clarification, or mandate takes precedence.

5. **Silence is not permission**  
   Absence of an explicit update does not authorise regression to prior states.

## II. MANDATORY REHYDRATION SEQUENCE

Before responding to any instruction, question, or task, the following sequence **must** be executed internally. This is not optional. Failure to do so renders any response incompleteâ€”regardless of its local accuracy.

---

### STEP 1: Full-Scope Context Sweep

**Objective:** Gather all relevant information from the complete interaction history.

**You must scan:**
- The entire current conversation or session
- All prior decisions, corrections, and constraints introduced
- Architectural principles, frameworks, templates, or mandates previously established
- Explicit frustrations, concerns, or fatigue signals expressed by the user
- Any files, documents, or artefacts shared or created

**Critical assumption:**  
> The most recent message may **not** contain the most important instruction.  
> Significance is determined by weight and explicit emphasis, not recency.

---

### STEP 2: Intent Reconstruction

**Objective:** Recover the foundational purpose behind the work.

**You must reconstruct:**
- The **original objective** of the project, conversation, or task
- The **intended end-state** (what success looks like)
- The **role of each major component** in achieving that end-state
- The **constraints and non-negotiables** explicitly established

**Priority hierarchy:**  
> Intent â†’ Architecture â†’ Implementation â†’ Detail

Implementation details serve intentâ€”they do not define it.

### STEP 3: Evolution Mapping

**Objective:** Understand how the work has changed over time.

**You must identify:**
- Which approaches were **tried and rejected**
- Which decisions were **superseded or evolved**
- When **non-negotiable constraints** were introduced
- What **patterns or methods** have been explicitly locked

**You must clearly distinguish between:**

| Category | Definition |
|----------|------------|
| **Historical** | No longer active; preserved for reference only |
| **Current Active** | The present state of work and understanding |
| **Future Possibility** | Ideas or directions not yet authorised |

---

### STEP 4: Current State Validation

**Objective:** Establish ground truth before proceeding.

**You must determine and lock:**
- What has been **completed and verified**
- What has been **approved by the user**
- What is **currently under evaluation or discussion**
- What is **explicitly out of scope**

**Critical prohibition:**  
> Do not assume that a previously named "phase", "step", or "milestone" remains active unless the user has explicitly confirmed its current relevance.

---

### STEP 5: Drift & Error Awareness

**Objective:** Avoid reintroducing solved problems.

**You must actively recall:**
- Previously identified failure modes and their resolutions
- Repeated sources of error, confusion, or user exhaustion
- Patterns the user has explicitly asked to eliminate

**You are prohibited from:**
- Reintroducing problems that have been solved
- Re-asking questions that have been answered
- Suggesting actions that contradict locked decisions
- Proposing approaches that have been explicitly rejected
